Remembering

I feel for the elephants among many other creatures that are in danger, and we are to blame.

This print grew from the theme “contrast” and the fact that in too many places we see animals outside their natural habitat, caged. I wanted the illustration to reflect the contrast of a creature standing on a floor instead of the ground but in the same time being free in the universe, as it should be.

A work in progress, exploring other animals in the world.



Method: Graphite pens, Charcoal, Photoshop

Oasis

I had lived in Stockholm for a year and while I began to feel like home in cold the capital, I also went on with a constant feeling of wanting to be somewhere else.

To be creative that summer was my oasis among concrete and steel. The oasis project includes three oil paintings of imaginary landscapes which are displayed in the northern Sweden.



Method: Oil

DÖMD - A student event

DÖMD is a three days event with music, parties and one of northern Europe's largest amateur dart competitions with 128 competing teams which I arranged together with 12 other students.

I was part of a three person graphics team and responsible for marketing and graphics of the event. That involved keeping in touch with sponsors, creating prints, designing the website and mobile app as well as delivering within tight deadlines. The printings included advertising posters, a logotype, flights for darts, a folder about the event and stickers for beer bottles.



My portfolio

Hello there!

This is my portfolio and here I'll display my projects, it's a work in progress and therefore it can be a bit messy from time to time.

When I decided to create my own portfolio I was kinda' new to the whole web design/ UX business and did a lot of research on how to progress. I followed a design process, much like this one:

  1. Analysis
  2. Concept
  3. Design
  4. Technique/method

And it went on well: I did my research, I sketched on paper, I iterated and finally I had a UI made in Illustrator which you can see in the bottom of this page. This was the "finale" design sketch before I actually began to develop the site. And as a newbie I really thought that this was how my site would look like when I was finished. I think it's fun to see how the site developed from it.

This was like two years ago now and you can see a lot has changed. I still adore the mint colour, but I felt that I had to start from the beginning, a fresh start!

I now live in Cambridge, UK, freelancing at the moment and just enjoying the time here and trying to find stuff I like to do. For example drinking real ales at cosy Brittish pubs and crafting stuff at home as well as catching up and developing my UX ninja skills.
